## Financial Sustainability Model

**Inputs:**
- Location sales estimate (based on foot traffic, demographics)
- All operating costs (COGS, labor, rent, utilities, insurance, misc)
- Royalty rate
- Initial investment

**Calculation:**
1. Monthly revenue - All expenses = Monthly profit
2. Monthly profit × 12 = Annual profit
3. Annual profit ÷ Initial investment = Annual ROI
4. Initial investment ÷ Monthly profit = Payback period (months)

**Thresholds for Viability:**
- Monthly profit > 5M won ✓
- Annual ROI > 25% ✓
- Payback period < 48 months ✓

---

## Red Flags in Financial Model

- Monthly profit < 3M won (too thin)
- ROI < 15% (not worth the risk)
- Payback period > 60 months (too long)
- Declining profitability trend (costs rising faster than sales)

---

## Korean Franchise Case Study

**Korean Convenience Store (2026):**
- Sales: 180M/month
- COGS (30%): -54M
- Labor (30%): -54M
- Rent (18%): -32M
- Royalty (5%): -9M
- Other (8%): -14.4M
- **Net Profit: -13.4M (LOSS)**

Same store, 2015 rent was 12%:
- Rent: -21.6M (instead of -32M)
- **Net Profit: -2.4M (still loss, but less)**

**Lesson:** Rent inflation transformed profitable franchise into unprofitable one.
