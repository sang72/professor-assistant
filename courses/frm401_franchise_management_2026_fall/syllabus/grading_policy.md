# Grading Policy
## FRM401 — Franchise Management (Fall 2026)

---

## Grading Overview

This document outlines the comprehensive grading policy for FRM401. Your final grade reflects both your academic performance on exams and assignments, as well as your professional conduct, engagement, and participation throughout the semester. The grading policy is fixed and applies equally to all students with no exceptions or special consideration.

### Overall Grade Breakdown

| Component | Points | Percentage | Description |
|-----------|--------|------------|-------------|
| Attendance | 20 | 20% | Consistent class attendance and punctuality |
| Midterm Exam | 30 | 30% | Comprehensive exam covering Weeks 1–6 |
| Final Exam | 30 | 30% | Comprehensive exam covering Weeks 8–14 (+ 20% cumulative from Weeks 1–6) |
| Assignments | 10 | 10% | Individual franchise analysis assignment |
| Attitude & Participation | 10 | 10% | Classroom engagement and professional conduct |
| **TOTAL** | **100** | **100%** | |

## Attendance Policy (20 points)

### Attendance Point Calculation

| Absences | Points Earned |
|----------|---------------|
| 0 absences | 20 / 20 points |
| 1 absence | 18 / 20 points |
| 2 absences | 15 / 20 points |
| 3 absences | 10 / 20 points |
| 4 absences | 5 / 20 points |
| 5+ absences | 0 / 20 points |

### Attendance Recognition Standards

- **On time (within 10 minutes of class start):** Counts as present
- **Late (10–20 minutes after class start):** Counts as 0.5 absence
- **Very late (more than 20 minutes after class start):** Counts as 1 full absence
- **Attending less than 40 minutes of class:** Counts as 1 full absence

### Attendance Verification

Attendance will be recorded at the beginning of each class session using [digital sign-in system / attendance roster / other method to be announced in class].

### Excused Absences

Students may request excused absence status for medical emergencies or family crises. Supporting documentation (medical certificate, official notice, etc.) must be submitted within **one week** of the absence. The instructor retains discretion to approve excused absence status.

### Critical Attendance Warning

Students with **5 or more absences** will be classified as at-risk for automatic failure and required to meet with the instructor for academic review.

## Exam Policy (60 points total)

### Midterm Exam (30 points)

- **Content Coverage:** All material from Weeks 1–6
- **Duration:** 75 minutes
- **Question Format:** 
  - Part A: Multiple Choice — 30 questions × 1.5 points = 45 points
  - Part B: Short Answer — 5 questions × 8 points = 40 points
  - Part C: Short Essay — 1 question × 15 points = 15 points
- **Scheduled:** Week 7, Session 3

### Final Exam (30 points)

- **Content Coverage:** Weeks 8–14 (primary focus, ~80%) plus 20% cumulative material from Weeks 1–6
- **Duration:** 90 minutes
- **Question Format:** 
  - Part A: Multiple Choice — 30 questions × 1.5 points = 45 points
  - Part B: Short Answer — 5 questions × 8 points = 40 points
  - Part C: Short Essay — 1 question × 15 points = 15 points
- **Scheduled:** Week 15, Session 3

### Common Exam Regulations

**Prohibited Items & Conduct:**
- Use of mobile phones, smartwatches, or any electronic devices is strictly forbidden during exams
- All unauthorized reference materials are prohibited
- Only materials explicitly approved by the instructor may be brought to the exam
- Academic misconduct (cheating, plagiarism, unauthorized collaboration) will result in a grade of zero for that exam and may lead to additional disciplinary action per university policy

**Make-Up Exams:**
- Make-up exams are only offered in cases of documented medical emergency or other unavoidable circumstances
- Written documentation must be submitted **within 3 days** of the exam date
- The instructor has discretion to approve a make-up exam in eligible cases
- Make-up exams may differ in format from the original exam

**Minimum Performance Standard:**
- Students who score below 40% on either exam must meet with the instructor for mandatory academic counseling

## Assignment Policy (10 points total)

### Assignment Breakdown

You will complete **1 individual assignment**, worth **10 points** toward your final grade.

### Assignment Scoring Method

Each assignment is graded on a 100-point scale, then converted to the course grading weight as follows:

**Conversion Formula:** Assignment Score × (10 ÷ 100) = Points toward course grade

**Example:**
- If you score 85 points on the assignment, your contribution to the course grade is: 85 × (10 ÷ 100) = 8.5 points / 10 points

### Late Submission Penalties

| Time Delay | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Submitted within 24 hours after deadline | 10% deduction from original score |
| Submitted 24–48 hours after deadline | 20% deduction from original score |
| Submitted 48–72 hours after deadline | 30% deduction from original score |
| Submitted more than 72 hours late | Automatic 0 points |

**No Re-submissions:** Once the deadline passes, re-submission is not permitted. Please review your work thoroughly before submitting.

**Missing Assignment:** Any assignment not submitted by the deadline is automatically scored as 0 points.

## Attitude & Participation (10 points)

### Evaluation Method

Your attitude and participation grade is determined through the instructor's observations throughout the entire semester, with a holistic assessment provided at the end of the course. There is no single exam or test for this component—it is based on the instructor's professional judgment.

### Mid-Semester Feedback

At Week 8, each student will receive informal feedback on their current estimated participation and attitude score, providing an opportunity to improve during the remainder of the semester.

## Grade Dispute Process

If you believe there is an error in your grade, you may initiate a formal grade appeal by following this process:

### Step 1: Deadline for Appeal

You have **exactly one week** from the date grades are posted to submit your grade appeal. Appeals submitted after this deadline will not be accepted.

### Step 2: How to Submit Your Appeal

Submit your appeal in writing via email to the instructor. Your appeal must include:

- Your full name and student ID
- The specific assignment or exam you are contesting (include question numbers if applicable)
- A detailed explanation of why you believe the grade is incorrect
- A reference to the answer key, rubric, or specific grading criteria
- The grade you believe you should receive and your justification

### Step 3: Instructor Review

The instructor will review your appeal within **one week** of receiving it.

### Step 4: Final Decision

The instructor's decision is final. You will be notified in writing of the outcome and any grade adjustment if approved.

### Step 5: Further Appeal

If you believe the appeal process was not conducted fairly or appropriately, you may escalate your concern to the Department Chair per university policy. Contact the Dean of Academic Affairs for guidelines on higher-level appeals.

## Academic Integrity & Course Conduct

All students are held to the highest standards of academic integrity. Any form of plagiarism, cheating, unauthorized collaboration, or academic dishonesty will result in:

- A grade of zero for the affected assignment or exam
- A report to the Dean of Students
- Possible further disciplinary action per university policy

### What Constitutes Academic Misconduct?

- Submitting work that is not your own (including using AI tools to generate content without proper disclosure)
- Copying answers from another student during an exam
- Unauthorized collaboration on individual assignments
- Failing to cite sources properly in written work
- Submitting the same assignment for multiple courses without instructor permission

### Proper Use of Sources

When consulting external sources for assignments, you must provide proper citations in APA format. Your instructor can help clarify expectations for citation and attribution.

## Grading Transparency & Communication

**Grade Posting:** All grades will be posted within 10 business days of submission.

**Grade Review:** You may request a private meeting to discuss your exam or assignment performance. Requests must be made within two weeks of grade posting.

**Communication:** All grade-related inquiries should be submitted in writing (email) to ensure a clear record.

## Rounding Policy

Final grades will be calculated to two decimal places and then rounded using standard rounding rules (e.g., 89.5 rounds to 90, which is an A-).
