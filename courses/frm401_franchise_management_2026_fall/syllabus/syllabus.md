## Course Description

This course explores the theory and practice of franchising as a pathway to wealth creation and entrepreneurial success. Franchising represents a unique business model that enables entrepreneurs to build rapid, scalable growth through strategic partnerships. Students will examine the franchise relationship from both franchisor and franchisee perspectives, analyzing how franchise systems create value through coordinated resource deployment, strategic management, and aligned incentives. The course balances theoretical frameworks with real-world applications, drawing on global case studies and comparative legal analysis. Throughout the semester, students will study the operational, financial, legal, and strategic dimensions of franchising, with particular emphasis on both U.S. franchise law and its Korean legal counterpart (가맹사업거래의 공정화에 관한 법률). By the end of this course, students will be prepared to evaluate franchise opportunities, design franchise systems, and understand the regulatory environment governing franchise relationships worldwide.

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

1. **Understand and Explain Franchising** — Define franchising and articulate how it differs from other business growth models; explain the fundamental principles that underpin successful franchise relationships.

2. **Analyze Franchisor-Franchisee Relationships** — Examine the rights, responsibilities, and interdependencies between franchise partners; evaluate the mechanisms that align incentives and create mutual value creation.

3. **Apply Franchise Legal Knowledge** — Understand core franchise legislation in both the U.S. and Korea; compare regulatory requirements and apply this knowledge to assess legal compliance in franchise scenarios.

4. **Evaluate Service Delivery Systems** — Assess how franchise systems design and deploy resources (real estate, marketing, operations) to meet customer demand consistently; analyze the strategic role of the service delivery system in franchise success.

5. **Conduct Financial and Transaction Analysis** — Perform financial analysis of franchise opportunities; use transaction analysis frameworks to assign tasks and responsibilities between franchisors and franchisees.

6. **Synthesize Global Franchise Strategy** — Design franchise expansion strategies for domestic and international markets; evaluate the adaptability of franchise systems across cultural and regulatory contexts.

7. **Apply Real-World Problem Solving** — Analyze contemporary franchise cases and scenarios; recommend strategic solutions that balance franchisor profitability with franchisee sustainability.

## Assignment Overview

### Assignment #1: Franchise Analysis & Strategy (Individual)
**Due Date:** End of Week 6

Students will choose ONE of the following options:

**Option A: Korean Franchise Brand Case Study**
Analyze a Korean franchise brand of your choice (e.g., Bagelz, Gongcha, Lotteria, etc.). Prepare a comprehensive analysis covering:
- Brand history and franchise model structure
- Franchisor-franchisee relationship dynamics
- Service delivery system and operational excellence
- Comparison with relevant U.S. franchise competitor
- Strategic recommendations for growth and sustainability
- Alignment with Korean franchise law requirements

**Option B: Global Franchise System Analysis**
Analyze a global franchise system operating across multiple continents. Prepare a comprehensive analysis covering:
- Franchise system architecture and core value proposition
- Adaptation strategies across different markets and cultures
- Service delivery system modifications by region
- Legal and regulatory navigation (focus on 2-3 key markets)
- Risk assessment and mitigation strategies
- Strategic recommendations for further international expansion

**Format:** 3,000–4,000 words; written in English; includes references to course materials and external sources
**Grading:** See detailed rubric provided in Week 5
