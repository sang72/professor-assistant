### Main Content (32 minutes)

**Segment 1: Weeks 1-3 Review (8 minutes)**

**Week 1: What is Franchising?**

Key concepts:
- Definition: Business model where franchisor allows franchisees to use brand and system for fees
- Why it exists: More efficient expansion than corporate ownership
- Who benefits: Franchisor (rapid growth), franchisee (proven system, brand), customers (consistency)

**Exam questions you might see:**
- "Define franchising. Why do companies choose franchising over corporate expansion?"
- "Compare franchisor benefits vs. franchisee benefits"
- "Give examples of franchises in Korea. Explain why each chose franchising."

**Week 2: Franchising as Entrepreneurship**

Key concepts:
- Franchisees are entrepreneurs using proven systems
- Krispy Kreme, Nene Chicken examples

**Exam questions:**
- "Why is franchising attractive to entrepreneurs without capital?"
- "What are the risks for entrepreneurs in franchising?"

**Week 3: Franchise Relationship Model**

Key concepts:
- Franchisor controls brand and system
- Franchisee controls local operations
- Alignment of interests is critical
- Contracts define relationship

**Exam questions:**
- "Explain the balance between franchisor control and franchisee autonomy"
- "What happens when franchisor and franchisee interests misalign?"

---

**Segment 2: Weeks 4-6 Review (8 minutes)**

**Week 4: Wealth Creation Mechanisms**

Four mechanisms (MEMORIZE THESE):
1. **Rapid Capital Growth** — franchisor uses franchisees' capital
2. **Risk Distribution** — each party bears manageable risks
3. **Systems Replication** — proven systems replicated efficiently
4. **Brand Building** — strong brands created through network multiplication

**Red Flags for System Failure:**
1. Emphasis on upfront fees over ongoing support
2. Oversaturation of locations
3. Weak/non-defensible systems
4. High franchisee failure rate
5. Franchisor financial instability
6. Inconsistent rule application

**Exam questions:**
- "Explain all four wealth creation mechanisms"
- "Apply mechanisms to new franchise scenarios"
- "Identify red flags in franchise cases"

**Week 5: SDS and Real Estate**

**Service Delivery System (SDS):**
- Integrated processes and standards
- Components: process standards, quality standards, equipment, training, supply chain, technology, brand standards
- Critical for consistency without heavy control

**Real Estate Strategy:**
- Location determines profitability
- Different strategies: high-traffic, density-based, destination, location-specific aesthetic
- Korean challenges: rising rents, oversaturation, building fragmentation

**Exam questions:**
- "What is SDS? Why is it critical for franchising?"
- "Compare different real estate strategies"
- "Evaluate location viability using profitability analysis"

**Week 6: Franchisee Selection & Monitoring**

**Selection Criteria:**
1. Financial capacity (minimum capital, net worth, credit)
2. Business experience
3. Industry knowledge and interest
4. Cultural fit
5. Management capability
6. Long-term commitment

**Monitoring Systems:**
- Financial (POS, royalties, profitability)
- Operational (store visits, mystery shopping)
- Customer feedback (reviews, surveys, complaints)
- Intervention protocols (coaching → training → penalties → termination)

**Exam questions:**
- "Should you franchise to [candidate profile]? Why/why not?"
- "Design a monitoring system for [franchise type]"
- "How would you intervene if franchisee struggles?"

---

**Segment 3: Weeks 7 Review (8 minutes)**

**Week 7: Advanced SDS**

**Standardization vs. Localization:**
- Core systems (must standardize): process, quality, equipment, training, brand visual, customer service
- Content (can localize): menu, pricing, store design, marketing, operating hours, staffing

**Technology Enablement:**
- POS systems (transactions, inventory, cashier accountability)
- Communication systems (portals, notifications, training)
- Challenges: cost, adoption resistance, integration
- ROI: 3-5 years

**Exam questions:**
- "Should a franchise standardize or localize this element? Why?"
- "Design an SDS system for [brand]"
- "How would you implement technology to enforce SDS?"

### Practice Problems (13 minutes)

**Practice Problem 1 (Multiple Choice Style - 2 min)**

Which is NOT a wealth creation mechanism?
A) Rapid capital growth
B) Risk distribution
C) Systems replication
D) Brand centralization

Answer: D (brand centralization is not a mechanism; brand BUILDING is)

---

**Practice Problem 2 (Short Answer Style - 3 min)**

"A Korean pizza franchise has 100 locations. Management wants to expand to 500 locations in 2 years. How would you evaluate this plan using the four wealth creation mechanisms?"

Your answer should address:
1. Capital growth feasibility
2. Risk distribution fairness
3. Systems replication capability
4. Brand building sustainability

---

**Practice Problem 3 (Case Analysis Style - 5 min)**

**Case:** "Bubble Tea Palace" franchise has:
- 250 locations in Korea
- Monthly sales: 15-25M per location
- Franchisee satisfaction: Declining (complaints about low profitability)
- Market: Oversaturated (too many bubble tea shops)

**Task:** Diagnose the problem and recommend solutions using concepts from Weeks 1-7.

Your analysis should include:
1. Which wealth creation mechanism is failing?
2. What SDS problems might exist?
3. Is this a real estate problem?
4. What's your recommendation to corporate?

**Sample Answer:**

**Problem Diagnosis:**
- Mechanism 3 (systems replication) is weak — bubble tea is commoditized, no defensible system
- Mechanism 4 (brand building) is saturated — market cannot support 250 identical shops
- Real estate strategy failed — oversaturation reduced profitability below sustainable levels

**Root Cause:** Corporate prioritized rapid expansion without ensuring franchisee profitability. Violated Week 4 principle: "Wealth creation requires BOTH sides to profit."

**Recommendations:**
1. Stop new openings (consolidate current network)
2. Close unprofitable locations
3. Develop stronger system differentiation (specialty bubble teas, unique positioning)
4. Improve franchisee profitability through:
   - Menu premium positioning (move away from commodity pricing)
   - Loyalty programs (increase customer lifetime value)
   - Operational efficiency (reduce costs)

### Final Tips (2 minutes)

**Exam Study Advice:**

1. **Memorize the Four Mechanisms** — they appear in every question
2. **Memorize Six Red Flags** — common exam question
3. **Know Selection Criteria** — used in many scenarios
4. **Understand Trade-offs** — growth vs. quality, standardization vs. localization
5. **Practice Case Analysis** — read the case, identify concepts, analyze, recommend

**Exam Strategy:**

- Read multiple choice carefully (many have subtle differences)
- For short answer: List key concepts, then explain
- For case analysis: Use frameworks (four mechanisms, six red flags, SDS components)
- Time management: 10 min multiple choice, 10 min short answer, 15 min case analysis, 5 min buffer

**What NOT to do:**
- Don't write everything you know (write only relevant content)
- Don't skip the "why" explanation (why does this matter?)
- Don't use Korean company examples if English examples work better
