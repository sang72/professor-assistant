### Main Content (32 minutes)

**Segment 1: Why Franchising is Regulated (7 minutes)**

Franchising creates an inherent power imbalance:
- **Franchisor** controls brand, system, training, support
- **Franchisee** invests capital, takes on debt, operates the business

Without legal protection, franchisors can exploit franchisees:
- Exaggerated profit claims
- Hidden costs
- Inadequate support
- Unfair contract terms
- Termination without cause

**Historical Context:** In the 1970s, many franchisees were bankrupted by misleading franchisors. Congress responded with regulation.

**Key Principle:** Franchise law aims to ensure full disclosure and fair dealing.

**Segment 2: The FTC Franchise Rule (8 minutes)**

**What is it?**
The FTC (Federal Trade Commission) Franchise Rule, first enacted in 1978, requires franchisors to disclose material facts to prospective franchisees.

**Key Requirements:**

**1. Franchise Disclosure Document (FDD)**
- Must be provided 14 days before franchise agreement signing or initial payment
- Contains 23 required items (detailed information)
- Updated annually

**2. The 23 Required Disclosures:**
1. Corporate structure and business history
2. Business experience of executives (5 years)
3. Litigation history of franchisor and executives
4. **Bankruptcy history** (last 10 years)
5. Initial and ongoing fees
6. Financing arrangements
7. Obligations to purchase from franchisor
8. Financing of franchisee obligations
9. Material restrictions on goods/services
10. Required participation by franchisee in operation
11. Termination, cancellation, renewal terms
12. Statistical tables on franchise transfers/failures
13. **Revenues/income data** from franchisees
14. Initial investment required
15. Restrictions on sale/transfer
16. Renewal, termination, or transfer
17. Earnings claims (if made)
18. **Financial performance information**
19. Company-owned locations and franchisee sales data
20. Names and addresses of franchisees
21. Transfer information
22. Dispute resolution mechanisms
23. Compliance history

**3. Earnings Claims Statements**
- If franchisor claims franchisees earn X dollars, must disclose
- Must be substantiated by actual data
- Many franchisors avoid earnings claims to avoid scrutiny

**4. Registration in Certain States**
- 14 states require registration of franchise offering
- Additional filing requirements
- California, New York, Illinois are most restrictive

**Penalties for Non-Compliance:**
- FTC enforcement
- Private lawsuits by franchisees
- Treble damages (3x actual damages) possible
- Injunctions
- Class action lawsuits

**Segment 3: State Franchise Laws (8 minutes)**

Beyond the FTC Rule, many states have additional protections:

**Key State Protections:**

**1. Unfair Termination Laws**
- Some states forbid termination without "good cause"
- Franchisee must be given opportunity to cure
- Franchisor must provide notice and time to remedy

**Example:** California requires "good cause" for termination (similar to employment law)

**2. Right of First Refusal**
- Some states require franchisor to allow franchisee to match competitor's acquisition offer
- Franchisor cannot just sell to third party at better terms

**3. Non-Compete Restrictions**
- Some states limit franchisor's ability to impose non-competes
- Balance between franchisor IP protection and franchisee freedoms

**4. Relationship Laws**
- Wisconsin and Minnesota have "franchise relationship laws"
- Require good faith and fair dealing
- Franchisee has some legal recourse if franchisor acts in bad faith

**5. Damages Protection**
- Some states award franchisee lost profits damages
- Allows franchisees to sue for lost earnings

**Segment 4: Real Cases - How U.S. Law Protects Franchisees (7 minutes)**

**Case 1: Misleading Earnings Claims**

A burger franchise claimed franchisees "typically earn $200,000 annually."

**Reality:** Average franchisee earned $40,000 after expenses

**Result:**
- FTC enforcement action
- 100+ franchisees filed class action lawsuit
- Settlement: Franchisees received $5M+ in damages
- Franchisor required to revise FDD

**Lesson:** Earnings claims must be truthful and substantiated

**Case 2: Unfair Termination**

A franchisor terminated a franchisee "for competitive reasons" (wanted company-owned store instead).

**Legal Challenge (California):**
- Franchisee sued for wrongful termination
- Court found termination lacked "good cause"
- Award: $2M in damages (lost profits)

**Lesson:** Termination must be for legitimate business reasons, not franchisee displacement

**Case 3: Hidden Costs**

A sub-sandwich franchise didn't disclose that franchisees were required to buy food only from franchisor supplier, which marked up prices 40%.

**Legal Challenge:**
- FTC investigated for deceptive practice
- Settlement: Franchisees refunded; franchisor required to disclose markups

**Lesson:** All material financial arrangements must be disclosed upfront

### Examples & Case Studies (13 minutes)

**Real U.S. Franchise Violations (Last 5 Years):**

**Wicked Horticulture (Cannabis) Franchise:**
- Claimed $500K annual profits with minimal work
- FTC found claims were "outrageous exaggerations"
- Enforcement: $1.5M restitution to franchisees

**Fast-Casual Restaurant Franchise:**
- Failed to disclose unit economics (50% of franchisees failing)
- Franchisee class action: $10M settlement

**Tech Support Franchise:**
- Required franchisees to purchase equipment only from franchisor at premium prices
- Not disclosed in FDD
- FTC action + private lawsuits: $8M+ damages

**Lesson for This Class:**
U.S. law provides strong franchisee protections through:
1. Mandatory disclosure (FDD)
2. State law protections (good cause, non-competes)
3. Private right of action (franchisees can sue)
4. FTC enforcement (government watchdog)

### 주요 내용 (32분)

**세그먼트 1: 프랜차이징이 규제되는 이유 (7분)**

프랜차이징은 내재적 권력 불균형을 생성합니다:
- 본부는 브랜드, 시스템, 교육, 지원을 통제합니다
- 가맹점은 자본 투자, 부채 부담, 사업 운영합니다

법적 보호 없이 본부는 가맹점을 착취할 수 있습니다.

역사적 배경: 1970년대에 많은 가맹점이 오도하는 본부에 의해 파산했습니다. Congress는 규제로 대응했습니다.

**핵심 원칙:** 프랜차이즈 법은 완전한 공시와 공정한 거래를 보장하는 것을 목표로 합니다.

**세그먼트 2: FTC 프랜차이즈 규칙 (8분)**

**그것은 무엇인가?**

FTC (Federal Trade Commission) 프랜차이즈 규칙, 1978년에 처음 제정, 본부가 미래 가맹점에게 자료 사실을 공시하도록 요구합니다.

**주요 요구사항:**

**1. 프랜차이즈 공시 문서 (FDD)**
- 프랜차이즈 계약 서명 또는 초기 지불 14일 전에 제공되어야 함
- 23개 필수 항목 포함
- 연간 업데이트

**2. 23개 필수 공시:**
1. 기업 구조 및 사업 이력
2. 임원진의 비즈니스 경험 (5년)
3. 본부 및 임원진의 소송 이력
4. **파산 이력** (지난 10년)
5. 초기 및 지속적인 수수료
6. 자금 조달 약정
7. 본부에서 구매할 의무
8. 가맹점 의무 자금 조달
9. 상품/서비스에 대한 물질적 제한
10. 가맹점의 운영 필수 참여
11. 종료, 취소, 갱신 조건
12. 프랜차이즈 이전/실패에 대한 통계 표
13. **가맹점의 수익/소득 데이터**
14. 필요한 초기 투자
15. 판매/이전에 대한 제한
16. 갱신, 종료 또는 이전
17. 수익 청구 (제시된 경우)
18. **재무 성과 정보**
19. 기업 소유 위치 및 가맹점 판매 데이터
20. 가맹점의 이름과 주소
21. 이전 정보
22. 분쟁 해결 메커니즘
23. 준수 이력

## SECTION 4: KEY NOTES

- **U.S. has strong franchisee protections**: Multiple layers (FTC, states, courts)
- **Disclosure is foundational**: FDD requirement prevents fraud
- **Private right of action critical**: Franchisees can sue, creating real deterrent
- **Damages can be substantial**: Treble damages and class actions make compliance essential
- **Some franchisors still violate**: Laws not perfect, but much better than no regulation
