## SECTION 2: FULL LECTURE SCRIPT (English)

### Opening (3 minutes)

Good morning! We've covered how to select franchisees. Now: How do you keep them successful?

This session is about monitoring—the systems that keep franchisees accountable and identify problems early.

Let's start.

### Main Content (32 minutes)

**Segment 1: Financial Monitoring Systems (8 minutes)**

Financial data tells the story. If a franchisee's sales are declining, it's an early warning sign.

**Daily Monitoring:**
- POS system data (real-time sales)
- Royalty payments (on-time or late?)
- Transaction count and average ticket size

**Weekly Monitoring:**
- Sales trend analysis
- Inventory turns
- Labor cost percentage

**Monthly Monitoring:**
- Profit and loss statement (estimated)
- Royalty payment check (did they pay on time?)
- Category performance (coffee vs. food, morning vs. afternoon)

**Red Flags:**
- Sales decline > 10% month-over-month
- Royalty payment more than 10 days late
- Labor costs exceed 35% of sales
- COGS costs exceed 35% of sales

**Action Steps:**
- If sales decline 10-15%: Schedule coaching call with franchisee
- If sales decline 15-25%: Visit location, conduct mystery shop, identify issues
- If sales decline >25%: Formal intervention plan required

**Example:** A coffee franchisee shows sales decline from 30M to 25M per month. This is a 17% decline—requires intervention.

---

**Segment 2: Operational Monitoring (8 minutes)**

Beyond financials, monitor operations:

**Store Visits:**
- Unannounced visits (at least quarterly)
- Checklist-based inspection
- Photo documentation
- Observation of customer service

**Inspection Checklist:**
- Cleanliness (floors, bathrooms, equipment)
- Product quality (coffee taste, food freshness)
- Staff attire and behavior
- Signage and merchandise displays
- Compliance with brand standards
- Inventory management

**Mystery Shopping:**
- Hire independent mystery shopper
- Mystery shopper orders typical items
- Evaluates: product quality, customer service, speed, accuracy
- Reports findings

**Technology Monitoring:**
- POS system login patterns (is manager present?)
- Inventory system entries (proper recording?)
- Communication system (responding to corporate messages?)

**Red Flags:**
- Failed health inspection
- Consistently poor customer service scores
- Inventory discrepancies (theft or waste?)
- Non-compliance with store standards

**Action Steps:**
- First occurrence: Coaching and retraining
- Repeated issues: Formal compliance notice
- Persistent issues: Franchise termination

---

**Segment 3: Customer Feedback Monitoring (8 minutes)**

What customers say is gold. Listen to it.

**Online Reviews:**
- Monitor Naver, Google, Coupang reviews
- Average rating should be 4.0+ (out of 5)
- Read negative reviews for patterns

**Customer Surveys:**
- Annual franchisee satisfaction surveys
- Monthly "how was your visit?" micro-surveys
- Track satisfaction trend

**Complaint Escalation:**
- If customer complains to corporate, escalate to franchisee
- Franchisee must respond within 24 hours
- Track complaint resolution rate

**Red Flags:**
- Average rating below 4.0
- Repeated complaints about same issue
- Complaints about cleanliness or food safety (critical)
- No response to customer feedback

**Action Steps:**
- Rating below 4.0: Investigate root cause
- Food safety complaint: Immediate inspection required
- Repeated service complaints: Retraining required

---

**Segment 4: Intervention Strategies for Struggling Franchisees (8 minutes)**

When you identify problems, what do you do?

**Level 1: Coaching (Non-Formal)**
- Schedule phone call with franchisee
- Discuss problem (sales, service, cleanliness)
- Provide specific suggestions
- Set 30-day improvement goal
- Follow up in 30 days

**Example:** Sales down 10%
"Your sales are declining. I've observed competitors opening nearby. Let's discuss marketing. Have you considered social media promotions? Price bundling?"

**Level 2: Training (Structured)**
- Franchisee or staff must attend corporate training
- Focus on specific skill gaps
- Hands-on practice
- Certification upon completion

**Example:** Customer service scores low
"Send your shift manager to our 2-day customer service training in Seoul next month. This will help your team improve."

**Level 3: Formal Compliance Notice**
- Written notice of non-compliance
- Specific issues documented
- Improvement timeline (usually 60 days)
- Consequences if not met (penalties or termination)

**Example:** Failed health inspection
"Your location failed health inspection. You have 30 days to correct all violations. We'll conduct follow-up inspection on [date]. Failure to comply may result in franchise termination."

**Level 4: Financial Penalty**
- Temporary reduction in franchisee's income
- Withhold percentage of royalties
- Reinvest in required training or equipment

**Example:** Repeated non-compliance
"Due to repeated non-compliance with cleanliness standards, royalties are temporarily reduced from 5% to 4% until standards are met."

**Level 5: Franchise Termination**
- Last resort
- Used only if franchisee refuses to comply
- Usually requires 90+ days notice
- Legal documentation required

**When is termination warranted?**
- Food safety violations
- Repeated SDS non-compliance despite interventions
- Financial fraud or royalty non-payment
- Criminal activity

### Examples & Intervention Scenarios (13 minutes)

**Scenario 1: Sales Decline (8 min discuss, 2 min present)**

A Starbucks franchisee shows:
- Month 1: 60M sales
- Month 2: 57M (5% decline)
- Month 3: 54M (10% decline)
- Month 4: 48M (20% decline)

Customer reviews: Previously 4.5 stars, now 3.8 stars

Your diagnosis needed. What's the problem? What do you do?

[Student groups discuss for 3 minutes]

Possible problems:
- Competitor opening nearby
- Poor customer service
- Product quality issues
- Staffing problems
- Franchisee not investing in marketing

Your intervention:
1. Schedule coaching call (Level 1)
2. Conduct store visit + mystery shop (diagnose issue)
3. Based on findings, provide specific recommendations
4. If issue persists after 30 days, move to formal training (Level 2)

---

**Scenario 2: Cleanliness Issue (2 min)**

Health inspector found: Dirty toilet, expired food in fridge, roaches in kitchen.

Franchisee's excuse: "I've been busy. My manager is incompetent."

Your action:
1. Immediate follow-up inspection required (within 1 week)
2. Formal Compliance Notice (Level 3)
3. Required manager training (Level 2)
4. If not corrected in 30 days, consider termination

---

**Scenario 3: Honest Franchisee Struggle (2 min)**

A franchisee calls: "Sales are down 25%. A competitor opened 500 meters away. I'm losing money. I need help, not penalties."

Your action:
1. Empathize (this is real)
2. Conduct location analysis (is the location truly oversaturated?)
3. Discuss options: price adjustments, menu changes, marketing, relocation, multi-unit strategy
4. If location is truly unviable, discuss exit options (release from franchise, help finding buyer)

Key point: Not all franchisee failures are due to franchisee fault. Market conditions matter.

---

**Scenario 4: Franchisee Refuses Monitoring (2 min)**

A franchisee refuses to provide POS data, refuses store visits, refuses mystery shopping.

Franchisee's argument: "It's my business. You're overstepping your authority."

Your action:
1. Review franchise agreement (monitoring rights)
2. Formal notice: "Monitoring is a franchise requirement"
3. If continued refusal: Franchise termination

This is a trust/control issue that must be resolved.

### Summary (2 minutes)

Monitoring systems have four components:
1. **Financial** (POS data, royalties, profitability)
2. **Operational** (store visits, mystery shopping, inspections)
3. **Customer Feedback** (reviews, surveys, complaints)
4. **Intervention Protocols** (coaching → training → compliance → penalties → termination)

Strong franchisors use all four and intervene early. This prevents small problems from becoming catastrophic.

## 한국어 번역 (Partial)

### 도입 (3분)

이제: 가맹점을 어떻게 성공적으로 유지하시겠습니까?

이 세션은 모니터링입니다—가맹점을 책임지게 유지하고 문제를 조기에 식별하는 시스템입니다.

### 주요 내용 (32분)

**세그먼트 1: 재무 모니터링 시스템 (8분)**

재무 데이터가 이야기를 말합니다.

**일일 모니터링:**
- POS 시스템 데이터 (실시간 판매)
- 로열티 지불 (정시 또는 늦음?)
- 거래 수 및 평균 티켓 크기

**주간 모니터링:**
- 판매 추세 분석
- 재고 회전율
- 노동 비용 비율

**월간 모니터링:**
- 손익 계산서 (예상)
- 로열티 지불 확인
- 카테고리별 성과

**위험 신호:**
- 월별 매월 10% 이상의 판매 감소
- 10일 이상 늦은 로열티 지불
- 노동 비용이 판매의 35% 초과
- COGS 비용이 판매의 35% 초과

**조치:**
- 판매 감소 10-15%: 가맹점과 코칭 전화 일정
- 판매 감소 15-25%: 위치 방문, 신비 쇼핑 실시, 문제 식별
- 판매 감소 >25%: 공식 개입 계획 필요

**세그먼트 2: 운영 모니터링 (8분)**

재정을 넘어 운영을 모니터링하세요.

**매장 방문:**
- 예고 없는 방문 (최소 분기별)
- 체크리스트 기반 검사
- 사진 문서화
- 고객 서비스 관찰

**신비 쇼핑:**
- 독립 신비 쇼핑객 고용
- 신비 쇼핑객이 일반적인 항목 주문
- 평가: 제품 품질, 고객 서비스, 속도, 정확성
- 결과 보고

**세그먼트 3: 고객 피드백 모니터링 (8분)**

고객이 말하는 것은 금입니다. 경청하세요.

**온라인 리뷰:**
- Naver, Google, Coupang 리뷰 모니터링
- 평균 평점은 4.0 이상이어야 함 (5점 만점)
- 패턴을 위해 부정적 리뷰 읽기

**고객 설문조사:**
- 연간 가맹점 만족도 설문조사
- 월간 "방문은 어땠나요?" 마이크로 설문조사
- 만족도 추세 추적

**불만 제기:**
- 고객이 본부에 불만을 제기하면 가맹점에 상향
- 가맹점은 24시간 내에 응답해야 함
- 불만 해결율 추적

**세그먼트 4: 어려움에 처한 가맹점을 위한 개입 전략 (8분)**

문제를 식별할 때 어떻게 합니까?

**레벨 1: 코칭 (비정식)**
- 가맹점과 전화 통화 일정
- 문제 논의
- 구체적인 제안 제공
- 30일 개선 목표 설정
- 30일 후 팔로우업

**레벨 2: 교육 (구조화)**
- 가맹점 또는 직원이 기업 교육에 참석해야 함
- 특정 기술 격차에 초점
- 실습
- 완료 시 인증

**레벨 3: 공식 준수 공지**
- 미준수의 서면 통지
- 문서화된 특정 문제
- 개선 타이프라인 (보통 60일)
- 충족하지 않을 경우 결과 (처벌 또는 종료)

**레벨 4: 재무 처벌**
- 가맹점 수입의 임시 감소
- 로열티 비율 보류
- 필요한 교육 또는 장비에 재투자

**레벨 5: 프랜차이즈 종료**
- 마지막 수단
- 가맹점이 준수를 거부할 경우에만 사용
- 보통 90일 이상 통지 필요

## SECTION 4: KEY NOTES

- **Early detection saves franchises**: Monitoring catches problems before they become catastrophic
- **Multiple data points matter**: Financial, operational, customer data all important
- **Intervention should be graduated**: Coaching before termination
- **Not all failures are franchisee fault**: Market conditions matter; franchisor must help

### Preparation Notes

- **Real examples**: Use actual monitoring scenarios from Korean franchises
- **Data focus**: Emphasize specific numbers (10% sales decline = action)
- **Intervention gradation**: Show progression from support to enforcement
- **Student engagement**: Ask about franchises they know that failed or struggled
