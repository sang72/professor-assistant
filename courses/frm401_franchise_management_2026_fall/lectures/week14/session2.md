## CU Vietnam Story

**Timeline:**
- 2018: Market research, partnership formation
- 2019: First store opened in Ho Chi Minh City
- 2020-2022: Rapid expansion (0 → 200 stores)
- 2023-2026: Continued growth (200 → 500+ stores)

**Strategy:**
1. **Entry Mode:** Company-owned first (proving concept)
2. **Product Adaptation:** Vietnamese products + Korean quality
3. **Supply Chain:** Import critical items, source locally where possible
4. **Pricing:** Lower than Korea, competitive with local options
5. **Marketing:** Focus on convenience + quality positioning

**Success Factors:**
- First-mover advantage (Vietnam had no major convenience chains)
- Strong operational system (CU's core competency)
- Understood customer needs (working professionals, students)
- Effective supply chain (leveraged Korean distribution expertise)

**Challenges Overcome:**
- Labor (trained Vietnamese staff in Korean-style efficiency)
- Supply (imported Korean snacks, sourced local produce)
- Competition (local shops emerging, but couldn't match CU quality)
- Currency (managed exchange rate volatility)

**Results (2026):**
- 500+ stores, largest convenience chain in Vietnam
- Profitable operations
- Expansion plan: 1,000+ stores by 2030

---

## Lessons from CU Vietnam Success

1. **Company-owned entry was smart** (proved market before franchising)
2. **Product adaptation essential** (Vietnamese items important)
3. **Supply chain advantage critical** (Korean quality + local adaptation)
4. **Local hiring and training key** (staff turnover managed well)
5. **Patience required** (first 3-4 years before profitability)

---

## Contrast: Why Nene Chicken Struggled in US

- Master franchising (less control)
- Poor US partner performance
- Limited brand recognition (no "Korean fried chicken" trend yet)
- Entry timing (too early, before Korean food trends)
- Result: Slow growth, limited presence

**Lesson:** Entry mode selection crucial for success.
