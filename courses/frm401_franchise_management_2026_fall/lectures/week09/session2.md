## Problem 1: Coffee Franchise Location Decision (10 min)

Two locations available. Both costs same upfront (200M won). Which is better?

**Location A (Gangnam):**
- Estimated monthly sales: 45M
- Rent: 12M/month
- Other costs: 21M/month (COGS, labor, utilities, insurance, misc)
- Royalty: 6%

Calculate: Net monthly profit

**Location B (Residential):**
- Estimated monthly sales: 25M
- Rent: 5M/month
- Other costs: 12M/month
- Royalty: 6%

Calculate: Net monthly profit

Answer: Location A = 45M - 12M - 21M - 2.7M (royalty) = 9.3M. Location B = 25M - 5M - 12M - 1.5M = 6.5M. **Choose Location A** (longer payback but higher profit).

## Problem 2: Royalty Rate Impact (8 min)

Franchisee has 30M monthly sales. Franchisor proposes raising royalty from 5% to 7%.

- Current royalty: 1.5M/month
- Proposed royalty: 2.1M/month
- Monthly profit impact: -0.6M

Is this sustainable? Answer: No. Franchisee profit drops from 3.5M to 2.9M. At 7%, margin becomes too thin.

---

## Problem 3: Break-Even Analysis (7 min)

Franchisee invested 180M won. Currently earning 3M/month profit. When break-even?

Break-even = 180M ÷ 3M = 60 months (5 years)

Is this acceptable? Borderline. Most franchisees expect 3-4 years.

- Problem 4: New franchisee with weak location gets 2M monthly profit. Option: (a) Accept low profit, (b) Invest in location relocation (50M), or (c) Quit franchise
- Problem 5: Franchisor wants to expand royalty from 5% to 8% to fund new marketing. Franchisees will lose 600K-1M/month individually. Is this fair?

## SECTION 3: KEY CONCEPTS

- Location determines profit more than royalty rate
- Payback period < 5 years is acceptable
- Profit margin of 10-15% is sustainable
- ROI of 30-50% is healthy
