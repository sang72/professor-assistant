# Week 14 · Session 1: International Franchising Part 2 - Regional Expansion

## FRM401 | 2026-Fall | Sangho Han

**Topic:** Korean franchises expanding to Southeast Asia and beyond

---

## Southeast Asia Opportunity

**Why Southeast Asia?**
- Geographic proximity to Korea
- Growing middle-class consumer market
- Similar food preferences (easier adaptation)
- Developing franchise market (less competition)
- Lower real estate costs

**Target Markets:**
- Vietnam: 100M people, growing consumer market
- Thailand: Developed market, significant Korean presence
- Indonesia: Large market, underdeveloped franchise infrastructure
- Philippines: Growing franchise market

---

## Korean Success Stories in Southeast Asia

**CU in Vietnam:**
- First foreign convenience store chain in Vietnam
- 500+ stores as of 2024
- Strategy: Company-owned + direct franchising
- Success factors: Product adaptation (local items), low prices
- Status: Thriving

**BBQ in Thailand:**
- Successful chicken franchise in Bangkok
- Growing to secondary cities
- Strategy: Direct franchising with local partners
- Success factors: Adapted spice levels, local marketing
- Status: Growing steadily

**Korean Cafe Brands in Southeast Asia:**
- Moderate success
- More competition from local cafes
- Differentiation: Korean aesthetic, quality consistency
- Status: Growing but slower than expected

---

## Challenges for Korean Franchises in Southeast Asia

**1. Product Adaptation**
- Local preferences matter (spice, flavor, portions)
- Must maintain Korean distinctiveness
- Risk: Over-adaptation loses Korean identity

**2. Supply Chain**
- Import costs for Korean ingredients
- Local supplier development required
- Quality control across borders

**3. Labor & Compliance**
- Different labor laws (wages, hours, benefits)
- Compliance complexity
- Staff training in new language/culture

**4. Competition**
- Local franchise systems already established
- International brands (McDonald's, Starbucks) already present
- Price competition intense

---

## Strategic Lessons

**Success Factors for Korean Franchises in SE Asia:**
1. Find underserved market segment (not direct competition)
2. Adapt product while maintaining Korean identity
3. Invest in local relationships and partnerships
4. Build supply chain before massive expansion
5. Realistic profit expectations (first 3-5 years slow growth)
