## Korean Brands Expanding Internationally (2024-2026)

**BBQ (Chicken):**
- Locations: Korea, US, China, Vietnam
- Strategy: Mix of direct (Asia) and master franchising (US)
- Challenge: Korean spice preferences don't match Western palates
- Solution: Offering multiple spice levels

**Nene Chicken (Chicken):**
- Locations: Korea, US, Southeast Asia
- Strategy: Master franchising in most markets
- Challenge: Brand recognition in new markets (not Korean name in home country)
- Status: Modest international presence

**Lotteria (Fast Food):**
- Locations: Asia primarily
- Strategy: Direct franchising within Asia, company-owned in key markets
- Advantage: Regional brand recognition
- Challenge: Not globally competitive like McDonald's

**CU (Convenience):**
- Locations: Korea, recently expanded to Vietnam, China
- Strategy: Company-owned stores for expansion (proving concept)
- Challenge: Different convenience store cultures in different countries

---

## Lessons from Korean International Expansion

1. **Korean products can go global** (BBQ chicken increasingly popular in US)
2. **Brand recognition critical** (Nene Chicken struggles as unknown brand globally)
3. **Localization necessary** (Spice levels, portion sizes must adapt)
4. **Partner selection crucial** (Master franchisee quality determines success)
5. **Competitive advantage needed** (Korean brands must be better than local alternatives)

---

## Future Outlook for Korean Franchises

**Promising:**
- BBQ, Korean fried chicken gaining international traction
- Korean food trends (K-Beauty, K-Pop) boost Korean brand recognition
- Younger global consumers interested in Korean products

**Challenging:**
- Korean brands often lack global scale vs. McDonald's, Starbucks
- Localization reduces distinctiveness (becomes "fusion" brand)
- Capital constraints limit international expansion
