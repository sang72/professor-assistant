### Main Content (32 minutes)

**Segment 1: Basic Franchisee P&L (8 minutes)**

A franchisee's income statement:

**Revenue:**
- Sales (coffee, food, services)

**Expenses:**
- Cost of Goods Sold (COGS): 30-35% of sales
- Labor: 25-35% of sales
- Rent: 10-20% of sales (location dependent)
- Utilities: 2-5% of sales
- Royalties: 4-6% of sales (franchise fee)
- Marketing: 1-3% of sales
- Equipment/maintenance: 1-3% of sales
- Insurance: 1-2% of sales
- Miscellaneous: 2-5% of sales

**Net Profit:** Sales minus all expenses

**Example:** 30M monthly sales coffee shop

| Item | Amount | % of Sales |
|------|--------|-----------|
| Sales | 30M | 100% |
| COGS | -9M | 30% |
| Labor | -9M | 30% |
| Rent | -4.5M | 15% |
| Royalties | -1.8M | 6% |
| Utilities | -0.9M | 3% |
| Marketing | -0.6M | 2% |
| Equipment | -0.6M | 2% |
| Insurance | -0.3M | 1% |
| Misc | -1.2M | 4% |
| **Net Profit** | **2.1M** | **7%** |

**Key Insight:** Even at 30M monthly sales, profit margin is only 7%. Small changes in any expense category significantly impact profitability.

**Segment 2: How Location Affects P&L (8 minutes)**

Same franchise, different locations:

**Location A (Premium, Myeongdong):**
- Monthly sales: 50M (high foot traffic)
- Rent: 15M (expensive location)
- Labor: 15M (need more staff for higher volume)
- Other costs proportionally higher
- **Net profit: 8-10M** (16-20% margin)

**Location B (Good, Residential):**
- Monthly sales: 30M
- Rent: 4.5M (moderate)
- Labor: 9M
- **Net profit: 2.1M** (7% margin)

**Location C (Poor, Low-traffic):**
- Monthly sales: 15M
- Rent: 3M (cheap location)
- Labor: 4.5M (fewer staff needed)
- Fixed costs per unit sales higher
- **Net profit: -1M** (LOSS)

**Lesson:** Location determines profitability more than any other factor.

**Segment 3: Royalty vs. Profit Relationship (8 minutes)**

Higher royalties mean lower franchisee profit. Franchisors must balance:

**Scenario 1: Low Royalty (3%)**
- 30M sales, 3% royalty = 0.9M royalty payment
- Leaves 11-12% net profit for franchisee
- Franchisor revenue per franchisee: 0.9M/month
- 500 franchisees = 4.5B/month franchisor revenue

**Scenario 2: Medium Royalty (6%)**
- 30M sales, 6% royalty = 1.8M royalty payment
- Leaves 7% net profit for franchisee
- Franchisor revenue per franchisee: 1.8M/month
- 500 franchisees = 9B/month franchisor revenue

**Scenario 3: High Royalty (8%)**
- 30M sales, 8% royalty = 2.4M royalty payment
- Leaves 3% net profit for franchisee
- Franchisor revenue per franchisee: 2.4M/month
- 500 franchisees = 12B/month franchisor revenue
- **Problem:** Only 3% franchisee margin is too thin. Franchisees struggle.

**Trade-off:** Higher royalty = higher franchisor revenue but lower franchisee success rate.

**Smart franchisors** balance this. Too high royalty kills franchisee profitability, leading to system collapse (see Cafe Bene, Week 4).

**Segment 4: Break-Even and ROI Analysis (8 minutes)**

Franchisees want to know: "How long until I recover my investment?"

**Initial Investment:**
- Store setup (furniture, equipment): 150-200M won
- Inventory: 10-20M won
- First month deposits: 5-10M won
- **Total:** 165-230M won

**Payback Period:** How many months until cumulative profit = initial investment?

**Example:** 30M monthly sales coffee shop with 2.1M monthly profit
- Initial investment: 200M
- Payback period: 200 ÷ 2.1 = 95 months (8 years)
- **This is too long.** Franchisees expect payback in 3-5 years.

**Better location example:** 50M monthly sales with 9M profit
- Initial investment: 250M
- Payback period: 250 ÷ 9 = 28 months (2.3 years)
- **This is acceptable.**

**ROI (Return on Investment):** Annual profit ÷ Initial investment

- 2.1M monthly = 25.2M annual = 12.6% ROI (on 200M investment)
- 9M monthly = 108M annual = 43% ROI (on 250M investment)

Franchisees typically expect 25-40% annual ROI.

### Examples & Case Studies (13 minutes)

**Case 1: Dunkin' Franchisee Economics**

Average Dunkin' franchisee:
- Initial investment: 350K USD (500M won)
- Average location sales: 1.5M USD/year (1.8B won/year = 150M/month)
- Operating profit: 200-300K USD/year (250-380M won/year)
- ROI: 50-60%
- Payback: 2-3 years

**Why is Dunkin' so profitable?**
- Strong brand (customers line up)
- Efficient operations (donuts + coffee only)
- High volume (1000s of customers daily)

**Case 2: Korean Cafe Franchisee Economics (2014)**

Cafe Bene franchisee:
- Initial investment: 150M won
- Average location sales: 20M won/month
- Operating expenses: 18M won/month (90% of sales)
- Operating profit: 2M won/month
- ROI: 16% (too low)
- Payback: 75 months (unacceptable)

**Why did Cafes fail?**
- Weak system (easy to replicate)
- Oversaturation (too many competitors)
- Low margins (commodity product)

**Case 3: CU Franchisee Economics**

Average CU franchisee:
- Initial investment: 100M won
- Average location sales: 150-200M won/month
- Operating profit (estimated): 4-5M won/month
- ROI: 50-60% (year 1)
- Payback: 2 years

**Why is CU profitable?**
- Efficient supply chain (no redundant middlemen)
- High-volume locations (convenience is product)
- Clear profit expectations from franchisor

### Summary (2 minutes)

Transaction analysis is about understanding franchise economics:

1. **P&L structure** determines profitability
2. **Location** is the biggest profit driver
3. **Royalty rate** affects both franchisor and franchisee
4. **Payback period and ROI** show franchisee viability
