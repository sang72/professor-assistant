## SECTION 2: FULL LECTURE SCRIPT (English)

### Opening (3 minutes)

Good morning! Welcome to Week 7, Session 2.

Without technology, a franchisor cannot monitor thousands of franchisees effectively. Technology is the backbone of modern franchising.

Today: How technology makes SDS possible at scale.

### Main Content (32 minutes)

**Segment 1: POS Systems (8 minutes)**

POS systems do more than ring up sales. They enforce SDS.

**Functions:**
1. Transaction recording (every sale logged with details)
2. Real-time sales data (corporate sees sales instantly)
3. Inventory tracking (automatic inventory updates)
4. Cashier accountability (tracks individual cashier sales)
5. Menu standardization (prevents menu changes)
6. Performance reporting (daily sales, hourly breakdown, category analysis)

**SDS Enforcement:**
- Item names standardized (prevents creative menu changes)
- Prices fixed (franchisees can't discount without approval)
- Portion sizes tracked
- Labor costs monitored

**Monitoring Benefits:**
- Real-time sales visibility
- Anomaly detection (triggers alert if sales abnormal)
- Labor cost tracking
- Inventory spoilage tracking

**Segment 2: Inventory and Supply Chain Systems (8 minutes)**

Franchisees must follow franchisor's supply chain. Technology enforces this.

**Functions:**
1. Authorized suppliers (system shows approved suppliers only)
2. Automatic ordering (system auto-orders when stock low)
3. Pricing control (central pricing, no local discounts)
4. Quality verification (supplier certifications tracked)
5. Ingredient tracking (chain of custody)

**SDS Enforcement:**
- Franchisees can't order from unauthorized suppliers
- Portion standardization (prevents shrinking to save costs)
- Expiration date tracking (prevents serving expired food)

**Monitoring:**
- Spoilage rates indicate waste/theft
- Order patterns reveal operational issues
- Cost per unit indicates efficiency

**Segment 3: Communication Systems (8 minutes)**

Technology enables constant corporate-franchisee communication.

**Systems:**
1. Franchisee portal (notices, updates, documents)
2. Push notifications (urgent messages)
3. Training platform (online modules, certification tracking)
4. Feedback loop (franchisees report, corporate responds)
5. Document management (centralized contracts and procedures)

**Monitoring:**
- Tracking franchisee compliance with viewing notices
- Training completion rates
- Response time to communications

**Segment 4: Challenges and ROI (8 minutes)**

**Challenges:**
- High implementation cost
- Technology adoption resistance
- Integration complexity
- Maintenance burden

**ROI Timeline:** 3-5 years

**Benefits:**
- Franchisor: Early problem detection, fewer failures, sustained revenue
- Franchisee: Better visibility, better decisions, higher profitability

**Example: CU's POS Upgrade (2020-2023)**
- 500+ billion won investment
- 13,000+ stores migrated
- Immediate: Reduced theft, real-time monitoring
- Long-term: Improved franchisee profitability

### Examples & Case Studies (13 minutes)

**Case 1: Starbucks Integrated Technology**

- POS system (real-time visibility)
- Inventory system (auto-ordering)
- Mobile app (integrated with POS)
- Store communication (push notifications)

Result: Problem identification within days, not weeks.

**Case 2: Korean Convenience Store Technology Race**

CU, GS25, Lotte Mart24 all investing heavily:
- Real-time POS
- Advanced inventory
- Delivery integration
- Customer AI analytics

Driver: Technology enables better franchisee support = better profitability.

**Case 3: Technology Gap Risk**

Korean coffee franchise refuses to implement POS ("too controlling").

Result: No effective monitoring. Quality declines. Sales drop. Blame game starts. System fails.

**Lesson:** Technology resistance leads to system failure.
