# Week 9 · Session 3: Case Analysis - Profitability Issues

## FRM401 | 2026-Fall | Sangho Han

**Task:** Analyze why Korean chicken franchise franchisees are struggling financially (2024-2026).

**Case Data:**
- Average sales per location: 60-80M/month
- Royalty rate: 6%
- Labor costs: 35% of sales
- COGS: 28% of sales
- Rent: 18% of sales (increased from 12% in 2020)
- Operating profit (calculated): 10M-14M/month initially, now 5-8M/month

**Questions:**
1. Calculate profitability for 70M sales location
2. Identify which cost increased most significantly
3. What's driving profit decline?
4. Should franchisees relocate or renegotiate?

**Key Issue:** Rising rents (from 12% to 18%) have compressed margins. This is a location/real-estate problem, not an SDS problem.

**Answer Framework:**
- Diagnose: Rent inflation reducing margins
- Root cause: Korean real estate inflation, oversaturation pushing rents up
- Solution: (a) Relocate to cheaper location, (b) Raise prices (risky), (c) Improve efficiency, (d) Renegotiate royalty
- Franchisor's role: Help franchisees navigate, don't blame them

**Study Notes:** This illustrates how financial analysis identifies system-level problems (Week 10 topic).
