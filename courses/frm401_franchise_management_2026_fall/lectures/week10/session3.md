# Week 10 · Session 3: Korean Franchisee Crisis Response Strategies

## FRM401 | 2026-Fall | Sangho Han

**Topic:** How should franchisors respond to profitability crisis?

---

## Crisis Response Options

**Option A: Cost Control (Franchisor helps reduce franchisee costs)**
- Renegotiate supplier contracts (buy in bulk, reduce COGS)
- Provide operational efficiency training
- Invest in technology to reduce labor needs
- Pros: Helps franchisees without franchisor sacrifice
- Cons: Limited impact if rent is main problem

**Option B: Revenue Enhancement (Franchisor drives customer traffic)**
- Invest heavily in brand marketing
- Promotional campaigns (loyalty, discounts, partnerships)
- Menu innovations
- Pros: Increases customer traffic and sales
- Cons: Requires significant franchisor investment

**Option C: Royalty Reduction (Franchisor shares burden)**
- Reduce royalty rate temporarily (5% → 3%)
- Tie royalty to profitability (sliding scale)
- Pros: Direct franchisee relief
- Cons: Reduces franchisor revenue

**Option D: Accept Consolidation (Franchisor acknowledges market maturity)**
- Allow franchisees to close unprofitable stores
- Reduce network size to sustainable level
- Improve profitability of remaining stores
- Pros: Healthier remaining system
- Cons: Acknowledges system failure

---

## Real Example: CU 2024-2026 Strategy

CU implemented Options A + B:
- Supplier renegotiation (reduced COGS 1-2%)
- Marketing investment (brand building in younger demographics)
- Technology (labor-saving POS and logistics)
- Result: Some profitability recovery, but challenges remain

---

## Lesson

**Franchisors must choose:** Support franchisees through crisis, or watch system collapse.

The 2024-2026 Korean franchise crisis tests whether franchisors remember Week 4: **"Both sides must profit."**

Franchisors who sacrifice for franchisees will survive. Those who don't will see system deterioration.
