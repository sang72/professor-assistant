## Case 1: GS25 Franchisee Revolt (2023-2024)

**Conflict:** Franchisees demanded royalty reduction from 5% to 3%

**Background:**
- Rising rents (15% of sales) made profitability impossible
- GS25 refused royalty cuts
- 2024: Organized franchisee action (media, regulatory complaints)

**Resolution:** GS25 offered operational support instead of royalty cut
- Supplier renegotiation (reduced COGS 1%)
- Technology investment (labor efficiency)
- Result: Partial relief, ongoing tension

**Lesson:** Refusing to adapt to market conditions breeds conflict

## Case 2: Korean Chicken Franchise (Name withheld for confidentiality)

**Conflict:** Franchisees sued over "unrealistic profit projections"

**Background:**
- Franchisor promised 200M annual profit
- Actual: 50-80M (franchisees took losses)
- Franchisees claimed fraud

**Resolution:**
- Settlement: Franchisor provided additional support
- Franchisees accepted lower profit expectations
- Some franchisees exited

**Lesson:** Overpromising damages trust permanently

---

## Solutions to Prevent/Resolve Conflicts

**Prevention:**
1. Accurate profit projections upfront
2. Regular financial transparency
3. Responsive support system
4. Franchisee councils (formal representation)
5. Clear dispute resolution process

**Resolution (when conflict occurs):**
1. Immediate dialogue (don't ignore)
2. Financial audit (understand real situation)
3. Fair burden-sharing (both sides sacrifice)
4. Implementation plan (clear actions)
5. Ongoing monitoring (ensure improvements)
