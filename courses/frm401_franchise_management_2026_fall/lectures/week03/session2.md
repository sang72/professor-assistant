## SECTION 2: FULL LECTURE SCRIPT

Last session, we introduced the Franchise Relationship Model. Today, we zoom in on the core relationship: franchisor and franchisee. How do they create value together?

The relationship is interdependent. The franchisor needs franchisees to execute the system locally. The franchisee needs the franchisor's system, brand, and support to succeed. When aligned, they are powerful. When misaligned, the partnership fails.

[SHOW SLIDE 1: Interdependence]

The franchisor's role is system design, quality control, brand management, innovation. The franchisor decides the menu at McDonald's. The franchisor designs the Starbucks store layout. The franchisor sets the brand standards.

The franchisee's role is local execution. The franchisee hires staff, manages daily operations, builds relationships with local customers. The franchisee is on the ground, making real-time decisions.

[WRITE ON BOARD: Franchisor = System Design + Quality Control | Franchisee = Local Execution + Relationships]

When these roles are clear and both parties understand the value they create for each other, the partnership is strong.

[TIME: ~15 min]

---

[SHOW SLIDE 2: Value Creation]

How do they create value? The franchisor reduces risk for the franchisee by providing a proven system. The franchisee reduces risk for the franchisor by assuming capital risk. The franchisor gains rapid scale without capital outlay. The franchisee gains access to a brand and system that works.

The customer benefits too. Consistency. Quality. Convenience.

[TIME: ~25 min]

---

[SHOW SLIDE 3: Potential Conflicts]

What can go wrong? The franchisor may demand too much control, leaving the franchisee with no autonomy. The franchisee may cut corners, damaging the brand for everyone. The franchisor may not provide adequate support. The franchisee may not follow the system.

Clear communication and aligned incentives prevent most conflicts.

[TIME: ~35 min]

---

[SHOW SLIDE 4: Alignment of Incentives]

How are incentives aligned? The franchisor profits when franchisees succeed. If a franchisee's sales increase, the franchisor's royalties increase. If the franchisee fails, the franchisor loses royalty income. This creates alignment. The franchisor wants the franchisee to succeed.

Similarly, the franchisee knows that following the system leads to success. The brand's reputation protects the franchisee's business.

When incentives are aligned, both parties work toward the same goals.

[TIME: ~42 min]

---

[ASK STUDENTS: Can you think of a situation where franchisor and franchisee incentives might conflict? What if a franchisee wanted to customize the product for local tastes?]

[PAUSE — wait for student responses]

Excellent questions. These are real tensions. Some franchisors allow customization. Others do not. The key is clear upfront communication about what is fixed and what is flexible.

Next session, we will deepen our understanding by studying the Pizza Hut and Domino's case. Both are pizza franchises, but they evolved differently. That case will show us how business model choices shape the franchisor-franchisee relationship.

Thank you. See you next session.
