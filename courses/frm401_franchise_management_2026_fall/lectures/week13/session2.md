**Topic:** Choosing the right international entry mode

---

## Entry Mode Decision Framework

**Questions:**
1. Do you understand the target market? (If no, use master franchisee)
2. Do you have capital for company-owned stores? (If yes, use this for proof)
3. Is the local business culture similar to home? (If no, adapt more)
4. How important is brand control? (If critical, use direct franchising)

**Decision Matrix:**
- Direct Franchising: High knowledge, high control needed, capital available
- Master Franchising: Low knowledge, willing to share control, capital limited
- Joint Venture: Large market, strategic partnership needed
- Company-Owned: Proof of concept needed, high capital available

---

## Risk Management in Global Franchising

**Risks:**
1. **Market Risk** — Product doesn't fit culture
2. **Partner Risk** — Master franchisee doesn't perform
3. **Currency Risk** — Exchange rate fluctuations
4. **Regulatory Risk** — Different laws, regulations
5. **System Risk** — SDS breaks down across cultures

**Mitigation:**
- Market research before entry
- Partner due diligence
- Currency hedging
- Legal compliance team
- Flexible SDS for cultural adaptation

---

## Korean Brand Case: Nene Chicken USA Entry (2018-2026)

**Strategy:** Master franchising (partner with US company)
**Rationale:** Limited US market knowledge, preference to share risk
**Challenges:** US partner didn't execute well, slow growth
**Lesson:** Master franchisee must be high-quality or entry fails
