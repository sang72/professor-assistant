### Main Content (32 minutes)

**Problem 1: Korean Cosmetics Franchise Going Global (8 min)**

"Beauty Seoul" wants to franchise in Japan. They must decide: What to standardize, what to localize?

**Current SDS (Korea):**
- Flagship products (Korean BB cream, ginseng serums)
- Store layout (minimalist Korean aesthetic)
- Staff training (2-week program)
- Customer service protocol (consultative selling)
- POS system (tracking recommendations and customer history)

**Japan Market Consideration:**
- Japanese customers prefer lighter, less coverage makeup
- Japanese stores typically narrow, vertical spaces (different from Korean wide layouts)
- Japanese customer service emphasizes politeness but less consultative approach
- Japanese staff labor laws different (different training requirements)

**Task:** Decide what stays, what changes.

**My Answer:**

**Must Standardize (Core SDS):**
- Ingredient quality standards (product safety critical)
- Training curriculum for cosmetics knowledge (customers need accurate advice)
- Core brand visual elements (Korean aesthetic identity)
- Technology system (POS and customer history tracking)

**Can Localize (Content):**
- Product mix (add lighter products for Japanese preferences)
- Store layout (adapt to Japanese space constraints)
- Service style (adjust from consultative to efficient)
- Marketing messages (Japanese language and cultural references)
- Staff uniforms (can reflect Japanese preferences within brand colors)

**Why this works:**
- Core product and brand identity maintained
- Local market needs addressed
- Consistency possible while respecting local culture

**Problem 2: Technology Resistance (8 min)**

A Korean food franchise has 500 franchisees. Corporate wants to implement new cloud-based POS system (cost: 200 billion won).

**Franchisee Concerns:**
- "It's too expensive for us individually"
- "It's too controlling—corporate watches everything"
- "Current system works fine"
- "What if cloud system goes down?"

**Corporate Arguments:**
- Better real-time visibility
- Reduced theft
- Better franchisee decision-making
- Long-term ROI

**Task:** How do you convince franchisees?

**My Answer:**

**Franchisor's Approach:**

1. **Financial Structure:**
   - Corporate covers 60% of hardware cost
   - Franchisees pay 40% over 3 years
   - Show 18-month ROI calculation

2. **Addressing Control Concerns:**
   - Transparency: Show exactly what data is collected
   - Benefit pitch: "System helps YOU make better decisions"
   - Privacy assurance: "Data is for your eyes and ours, not government"

3. **Risk Mitigation:**
   - Redundant servers (system downtime risk eliminated)
   - Local backup on franchisee location (offline capability)
   - 24/7 support hotline

4. **Pilot Program:**
   - 50 early-adopter franchisees
   - Results documented and shared
   - Peer pressure from success cases

5. **Mandatory Timeline:**
   - 3-year mandatory transition
   - Non-compliance terminates franchise

**Result:** Mix of carrot (financial help, benefits) and stick (mandatory timeline).

**Problem 3: SDS Breakdown Case (8 min)**

A Korean coffee franchise experienced SDS collapse:
- Menu drift (franchisees adding creative drinks)
- Quality drift (different brewing times by location)
- Price drift (franchisees discounting locally)
- Service drift (different customer service standards)

**Why Did It Happen?**
- No POS system to enforce menu and pricing
- Training insufficient to enforce brewing standards
- No mystery shopping to evaluate service
- Corporate staff too small to visit locations often

**Task:** Redesign SDS system to prevent this.

**My Answer:**

**Immediate (Months 1-3):**
1. Implement POS system with fixed menus/prices
2. Implement mystery shopping (monthly)
3. Audit all locations (video record standard procedures)

**Medium-term (Months 3-6):**
1. Retrain all franchisees on brewing standards
2. Install backup brewing procedure documentation at each location
3. Establish communication platform for daily corporate-franchisee updates

**Long-term (6+ months):**
1. Analyze data to identify non-compliant franchisees
2. Implement intervention hierarchy (coaching → training → penalties → termination)
3. Develop customer feedback system (reviews monitored for quality issues)

**Cost:** ~500 million won year 1, ongoing 200M annually

**ROI:** Consistent quality → customer loyalty → sales stability

### Group Activity (13 minutes)

Work in groups. Solve this problem:

**"Noodle King" Expansion Challenge**

Korean noodle franchise with 200 locations wants to expand to Vietnam.

**Decision Points:**

1. **Product:** Keep Korean noodles exactly as is, or adapt broth/spices for Vietnamese palates?

2. **Store Design:** Keep Korean minimalist design, or add Vietnamese cultural elements?

3. **Technology:** Implement POS system from Korea, or partner with local tech company?

4. **Staffing:** Train in Korea then deploy, or train locally?

5. **Supply Chain:** Source from Korea, or local Vietnam suppliers?

**Each group proposes:**
- Decision for each point
- Reasoning
- Risks and benefits

[Groups discuss 8 min, present 5 min]

### Summary (2 minutes)

Key framework:
- **Core SDS**: Don't compromise
- **Customizable content**: Adapt for market
- **Technology**: Enabler not constraint
- **Risk management**: Pilot before rollout
