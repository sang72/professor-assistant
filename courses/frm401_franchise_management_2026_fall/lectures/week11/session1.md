# Week 11 · Session 1: Franchisee-Franchisor Conflict & Dynamics

## FRM401 | 2026-Fall | Sangho Han

**Topic:** Why do franchisee-franchisor relationships break down?

---

## Key Conflict Sources

**1. Financial Mismatch**
- Franchisor expects 5% royalty; franchisee sees 90% of it going to rent
- Franchisee feels franchisor profit comes at franchisee's expense
- Franchisor sees royalty as "reasonable" fee for system

**2. Support Disparity**
- Franchisee expects intensive support; franchisor provides basic
- Franchisee feels abandoned when struggling
- Franchisor feels franchisee should be self-sufficient

**3. Decision Authority Disputes**
- Franchisor mandates menu; franchisee wants autonomy
- Franchisor enforces standards; franchisee sees as control

**4. Information Asymmetry**
- Franchisor has system-wide data; franchisee has only local data
- Franchisor makes decisions based on network health; franchisee sees own struggle

---

## Real Conflict Examples (Korean, 2024-2026)

**Convenience Store Conflict:**
- Franchisees: "Rent too high, profits impossible. Reduce royalty."
- Franchisor: "This is competitive market rate. Improve efficiency."
- Result: Lawsuits, media coverage, regulatory investigation

**Coffee Franchise Conflict:**
- Franchisees: "You oversaturated market. Now closing stores."
- Franchisor: "We provided system. You chose bad location."
- Result: System collapse, bankruptcy

---

## Conflict Resolution Paths

**Path 1: Win-Win (Rare)**
- Both sides make concessions
- CU and franchisees working toward operational efficiency together
- Result: Sustainable improvements

**Path 2: Franchisor Wins**
- Franchisor maintains position; franchisees accept losses
- Result: Franchisee resentment, system deterioration, lawsuits

**Path 3: Franchisee Wins**
- Franchisees demand changes; franchisor capitulates
- Result: Franchisor weakened, system may collapse

**Path 4: System Collapse**
- No resolution; both sides lose
- Cafe Bene scenario
- Result: Bankruptcy, closure

---

## Prevention Strategies

1. **Clear Communication:** Regular franchisor-franchisee dialogue
2. **Transparent Data:** Share financial data with franchisees
3. **Fair Burden-Sharing:** When crisis hits, share sacrifice
4. **Support Enhancement:** Franchisor invests in franchisee success
5. **Governance:** Franchisee associations with formal voice
